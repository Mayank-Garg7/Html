Colors and Layout

HTML provides ways to style content and structure layouts. You can define colors using six methods:

  • Color Name: Predefined names like red, blue, green.
  • Hexadecimal Value: A combination of 6 digits, e.g., #FF5733.
  • RGB: A combination of red, green, and blue, e.g., rgb(255, 87, 51).
  • RGBA: RGB with an alpha (opacity) value, e.g., rgba(255, 87, 51, 0.5).
  • HSL: Hue, Saturation, and Lightness, e.g., hsl(16, 100%, 60%).
  • HSLA: HSL with an alpha value, e.g., hsla(16, 100%, 60%, 0.5).

Div: Used to group content together for styling and layout purposes.


Section Title

Section: Represents a thematic grouping of content, usually with a heading.


Article Title

Article: Used for independent, reusable content such as blog posts or news articles.


Aside: Often used for supplementary content like sidebars, notes, or advertisements.
